# Problem Notes

## Selected Case
Gravity — A Peer-to-Peer Student Marketplace. The problem is that RSU students who move in or out struggle to sell or buy used items (furniture, appliances, textbooks, dorm essentials) because the activity is spread across scattered, unsearchable Facebook and LINE groups.

## Target Respondents
| Respondent Type | Why This Respondent Matters | Planned Number | Actual Number |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Graduating / leaving students | Have the most items to clear quickly before they leave | 5 | 4 |
| New / first-year students (buyers) | Need cheap furniture and essentials when they arrive | 5 | 4 |
| International students | Move in and out across countries and cannot keep large items | 3 | 3 |
| Dorm residents | Change rooms each year and buy/sell small items frequently | 4 | 4 |

## Observed / Reported Problem
When students move in or out of RSU, they need to sell, give away, or buy used items quickly. Today this happens in general Facebook and LINE buy-sell groups, through friends, or on noticeboards. Listings are buried in the feed, there is no way to search by item type or filter, posts disappear within a day, and the audience is limited. Sellers under time pressure often give items away or throw them out, and buyers cannot find affordable used items in time and end up buying new.

## Current Alternatives or Workarounds
| Workaround | Who Uses It? | Weakness / Pain | Evidence Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Facebook buy-sell groups | Most students | Listings buried, no search/filter, posts disappear quickly | Responses R001-R015 |
| LINE / dorm and class group chats | Dorm residents, classmates | Messages scroll away, very small audience | Responses R002, R008, R012 |
| Word of mouth / asking friends | Almost everyone | Limited reach, slow, depends on luck | Responses R004, R010 |
| Leaving items behind or throwing away | Movers in a hurry, leaving students | Total loss of value and creates waste | Responses R003, R007, R014 |
| Physical noticeboards | A few students | Low visibility, manual, easy to miss | Responses R011 |

## Repeated Pain Points
| Pain Point | Number of Mentions | Example Evidence | Severity |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Scattered, unsearchable channels | 11 | "I had to scroll the Facebook group for an hour to find a desk" | High |
| Listings disappear / get buried quickly | 5 | "My post was buried by the next day and nobody saw it" | Medium |
| Time pressure when moving out | 6 | "I only had three days before my flight to sell everything" | High |
| Trust / safety meeting strangers | 3 | "I felt unsafe sharing my Facebook and meeting a stranger alone" | Medium |
| Usable items given away or discarded | 7 | "I ended up throwing away a good desk because nobody bought it" | High |

## Consequences
| Consequence | Example | Evidence Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Lost money (items given away or discarded) | Threw away a desk and chair worth ~2,000 THB | Moderate |
| Wasted time posting and searching | 30-90 minutes per item across several groups | Moderate |
| Buyers overpay for new items | Bought a new fan because no used one was found in time | Moderate |
| Usable items wasted (environmental cost) | Working appliances left behind in the dorm | Moderate |

## Notes for MVP Direction
The team should consider: a single searchable listings page with item categories, photos, and a filter by item type, price, and date; a way to surface listings during move-in/move-out periods when demand spikes; and a safe contact method (in-app message or reveal-contact-after-both-agree) to address the trust and safety concern, especially for students meeting strangers. Early evidence suggests focusing first on higher-value items (furniture, appliances, textbooks) rather than every small item.
