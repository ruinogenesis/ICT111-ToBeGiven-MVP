# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title
Gravity — A Peer-to-Peer Student Marketplace

## Target Users Studied
RSU students who buy or sell used items around moving in or out of university: graduating/leaving students, new and continuing students (buyers), international students, and dorm residents.

## Number of Responses / Interviews
15 early responses collected through a short survey and brief interviews (sample/early-stage evidence, recorded in /data/raw-responses.xlsx).

## Main Repeated Problem
Buying and selling used items at RSU is spread across scattered, unsearchable Facebook and LINE groups. There is no single searchable place, listings disappear quickly, and the audience is limited — so sellers under time pressure lose time and money (often giving items away or discarding them), and buyers cannot find affordable used items in time.

## Main Current Workarounds
- Facebook buy-sell groups (most common)
- LINE and dorm/class group chats
- Word of mouth and asking friends
- Physical noticeboards
- Giving items away or throwing them out when nothing sells in time

## Strongest Evidence Found
- Scattered, unsearchable channels were mentioned by 11 of 15 respondents — the most consistent pattern.
- Measurable time loss: respondents reported spending roughly 30–90 minutes per item across multiple groups.
- Real value lost: 7 of 15 respondents had given away or discarded usable items (e.g. a desk, working appliances) because they could not sell in time.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings
- Willingness to adopt a dedicated platform is not yet behaviorally tested — respondents expressed interest, but interest is not the same as real use (we have no sign-ups or completed listings yet).
- Demand for buying/selling small, low-value items is unclear and may be weak.
- The trust/safety concern needs deeper probing to understand what would make students feel safe completing a deal.

## What We Must Revise
- Narrow the focus to higher-value items (furniture, appliances, textbooks) and to move-in/move-out periods, rather than every item all year.
- Add a safer contact step (in-app message or reveal-contact-after-both-agree) instead of immediately exchanging personal accounts.
- Plan an adoption test in a later lab to move from "interest" to real behavior.

## Updated Problem Statement
RSU students who move out at the end of a term — especially graduating and international students — lose time and money trying to sell used furniture, appliances, and textbooks through scattered, unsearchable Facebook and LINE groups, and often give items away or discard them; at the same time, new and continuing students struggle to find affordable used versions of those items in time and end up buying new.
